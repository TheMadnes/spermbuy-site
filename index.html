FAQ
Common Questions
Everything you need to know before getting started.
Is buying sperm legal?
Yes. Purchasing donor sperm is fully legal in all 50 U.S. states and most countries worldwide. SpermBuy operates through FDA-registered tissue banks with full regulatory compliance. All transactions include legally binding contracts reviewed by reproductive law attorneys.
How are donors screened?
Every donor undergoes a comprehensive screening process: physical examination, infectious disease testing (FDA-mandated), genetic carrier screening for 300+ conditions, psychological evaluation, family medical history review (3+ generations), and criminal background check.
Can the donor claim parental rights?
No. All legal contracts explicitly terminate any parental rights of the donor. Under the Uniform Parentage Act and state-specific laws, sperm donors who donate through licensed facilities have no legal claim to parental rights.
How is the sperm shipped?
Samples are shipped in specialized cryogenic tanks using liquid nitrogen vapor, maintaining temperatures below -150¬∞C. Tanks are designed for 7-10 day holding time. We ship nationwide with full tracking and insurance.
What's the difference between anonymous and open-ID?
Anonymous donors remain unidentifiable. Open-ID donors agree that their identity can be released to donor-conceived children once they turn 18. Both options include full medical and genetic profiles.
